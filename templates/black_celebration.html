Black Celebration (1986)
Released in 1986, Black Celebration marked a shift toward darker, more atmospheric sounds for Depeche Mode. The album combines brooding synths, intricate electronic rhythms, and introspective lyrics, exploring themes of alienation, desire, and social commentary. Standout tracks like “Stripped” and “A Question of Time” highlight the band’s skill at blending mood and melody, making Black Celebration one of their most influential and critically acclaimed albums.
